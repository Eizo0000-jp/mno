## How to Set Up Roaming Before Your Trip

Setting up overseas roaming on Rakuten Mobile is straightforward, but there are a few things to confirm in advance so you don't land abroad without connectivity.

### Step 1: Enable Data Roaming on Your Phone

Before you leave Japan, go into your phone's settings:

- **iPhone**: Settings → Mobile Data → Mobile Data Options → toggle on "Data Roaming"
- **Android**: Settings → Connections → Mobile Networks → toggle on "Data Roaming"

This is a simple step but easy to forget. If your phone doesn't have roaming toggled on, you won't connect even if Rakuten's roaming is active.

### Step 2: Check Your Country Is Covered

Rakuten Mobile's roaming coverage changes periodically. Before traveling, visit the [official Rakuten Mobile international roaming page](https://network.mobile.rakuten.co.jp/service/global/roaming/) or check the "My Rakuten Mobile" app to confirm your destination is on the list. Most major countries in Asia, Europe, North America, and Oceania are covered.

### Step 3: Monitor Your 2GB Usage

Once abroad, you can check your remaining roaming data through the **My Rakuten Mobile app**. The 2GB resets at the start of each calendar month, so if you're on a trip that spans two months, you'll technically get access to a fresh 2GB as the month rolls over.

### Step 4: Use Wi-Fi Calling and Messaging Apps

Even at 128kbps after the 2GB limit, you can use LINE, WhatsApp, iMessage, and similar messaging apps comfortably. For voice calls, Rakuten Link (Rakuten Mobile's free calling app) also works over Wi-Fi abroad, so you can call Japanese numbers for free even when overseas.

## Is 2GB of Roaming Actually Enough?

This is the honest question worth addressing. For some people, yes — and for others, not quite.

**2GB is probably enough if you:**
- Travel for short trips (3–7 days) and mainly use messaging apps
- Have access to Wi-Fi at hotels or family homes
- Don't need to stream video content while abroad

**2GB might not be enough if you:**
- Work remotely while traveling and need constant connectivity
- Travel frequently for 2+ weeks at a time
- Rely heavily on video calls or streaming data

In those cases, you'd either want to supplement with a local SIM or a pocket Wi-Fi abroad. But for the typical foreign resident visiting family once or twice a year, the 2GB free roaming is a genuine perk that saves real money.
