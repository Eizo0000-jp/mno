## Why Foreign Residents Are Leaving SoftBank

SoftBank isn't a bad carrier. It has strong coverage, solid customer support, and plenty of plan options. But for many foreign residents — especially those watching their monthly expenses — the pricing can feel hard to justify.

A typical SoftBank unlimited plan often costs **¥7,000–¥9,000 or more per month**, before any discounts. Even with family bundle discounts or promotional pricing, many users end up paying significantly more than they need to for data they may not fully use.

If you travel internationally, work remotely, or simply want to reduce fixed expenses in Japan, it makes sense to at least compare your options.

## The Cons of Switching from SoftBank to Rakuten Mobile

### ⚠️ Network Coverage Can Be Patchy in Rural Areas

Rakuten Mobile's own network (Band 3) is strongest in urban areas — Tokyo, Osaka, Nagoya, and other major cities. In rural areas or mountainous regions, it falls back on au's network (through a roaming agreement), which may result in slightly reduced speeds or limited coverage.

If you live in a rural area or frequently travel through the Japanese countryside, this is worth considering carefully.

### ⚠️ Customer Support May Be Harder for Non-Japanese Speakers

SoftBank has more developed multilingual customer support infrastructure. Rakuten Mobile's support is primarily in Japanese, which can be a barrier if you're not yet comfortable with the language. However, many foreigners navigate this successfully using translation apps or with the help of Japanese-speaking friends.

### ⚠️ Point Payout Has a Delay

As mentioned, the referral points are distributed in installments starting **4 months after the referral login month**. This isn't a dealbreaker, but it's good to go in with realistic expectations — you won't receive all 14,000 points immediately.

### ⚠️ Some Devices May Need Configuration

If you're bringing your SoftBank handset to Rakuten Mobile, most modern unlocked smartphones will work fine. However, SIM-locked devices (older models) may need to be unlocked first. Check compatibility on Rakuten Mobile's official website before switching.

## How to Switch: MNP Process Overview

Switching via MNP (Mobile Number Portability) lets you keep your current phone number. Here's a simplified overview of the process:

1. **Get your MNP transfer number (MNP予約番号)** from SoftBank — via the My SoftBank app, phone, or in-store
2. **Sign up for Rakuten Mobile** using the referral link (MNP option)
3. **Insert your new SIM or eSIM** and complete the activation
4. Your number will transfer automatically, usually within minutes for eSIM

The whole process can often be done online in under 30 minutes if you have everything ready.
