## How Are the Points Paid Out?

Before you get too excited, it's important to understand the point payout schedule:

- Points are **not paid all at once**
- They are distributed in **3 installments**
- Payments begin **4 months after your referral login month**

So if you sign up using a referral link in, say, June, you can expect to start receiving points around October. The three installments are spread out over a period of time after that. It's still a great deal — just don't plan on spending those points the week after you sign up.

This structure is worth knowing for budgeting purposes, especially if you're planning to use those points toward Rakuten shopping, travel bookings, or other services in the Rakuten ecosystem.

## Things to Keep in Mind as a Foreign Resident

Switching mobile carriers in Japan as a non-Japanese resident does come with a few extra steps to be aware of:

- **Identification**: You'll need your residence card (在留カード) and a payment method (credit card recommended)
- **Language**: The Rakuten Mobile signup process is available in Japanese; having a translator app handy or going through a bilingual guide helps
- **MNP reservation number**: Before porting your number, you'll need to request an MNP reservation number (MNP予約番号) from your current carrier — this is valid for a limited time, so don't wait too long after obtaining it
