## Why Foreigners in Japan Struggle to Find the Right SIM

Switching carriers in Japan isn't always straightforward, especially if Japanese isn't your first language. Major carriers like NTT Docomo, au, and SoftBank offer extensive coverage and support, but their plans are often expensive and their contracts can be confusing — even for Japanese natives.

MVNOs (Mobile Virtual Network Operators) like IIJmio or OCN are cheaper, but they come with data caps and slower speeds during peak hours. For many foreign residents who rely on their smartphones for navigation, video calls back home, work communications, and social media, these limitations can be genuinely frustrating.

That's where Rakuten Mobile fills the gap.

## What to Prepare Before Switching

Switching to Rakuten Mobile is relatively painless if you prepare a few things in advance:

1. **MNP Transfer Number (MNP予約番号):** If you're switching from another carrier, request this from your current provider before applying.
2. **My Number Card or Residence Card:** For identity verification, you'll need a valid form of ID. Foreign residents can use their Zairyu Card (在留カード).
3. **Rakuten ID:** You'll need a Rakuten account. If you don't have one, it's free to create and takes just a few minutes.
4. **Compatible Phone:** Check that your current device supports Rakuten Mobile's bands. Most modern smartphones do, but it's worth confirming on their official website.
