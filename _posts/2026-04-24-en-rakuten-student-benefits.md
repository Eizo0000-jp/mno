Living in Japan as a student — whether you're here on a study abroad program, a language school enrollment, or a full university degree — means managing your budget carefully. Rent, food, transportation, and school fees all add up fast. One area where many students unknowingly overpay is their monthly phone bill.

If you're currently on a major carrier like SoftBank, au, or Docomo, there's a good chance you're spending ¥5,000 to ¥8,000 or more every month just for mobile service. Rakuten Mobile offers a compelling alternative, especially when you sign up through an employee referral link that unlocks a bonus point campaign unavailable through the standard website.

This article breaks down exactly how Rakuten Mobile can benefit students in Japan — including how much you can earn in points, what the monthly plan looks like, and how to get started.

## What Makes Rakuten Mobile a Smart Choice for Students?

Rakuten Mobile operates on a single unlimited data plan priced at **¥3,278 per month (tax included)**. For students who rely on mobile data for online classes, research, streaming, and staying connected with family back home, this simplicity is genuinely valuable.

Compare that to the ¥6,000–¥8,000 you might pay on a traditional carrier for a similar amount of data, and the savings are immediate.

One often-overlooked benefit for international students is the **Rakuten Link app**, which allows completely free domestic calls in Japan. For foreigners navigating Japanese bureaucracy, this can save more than you'd expect.

### When Do the Points Arrive?

Points are paid in **three installments**, beginning **four months after the month in which you complete your referral login**. This is worth knowing in advance so you can plan accordingly — it's not an instant reward, but it does arrive reliably across the payout schedule.

## Is Rakuten Mobile Network Coverage Good Enough for Students?

This is a fair concern, and it's worth addressing honestly. Rakuten Mobile has been building out its own network infrastructure across Japan, and coverage in major urban areas — Tokyo, Osaka, Nagoya, Kyoto, Fukuoka — is solid and continues to improve.

In rural areas or on certain underground train lines, you may occasionally experience weaker signal. However, Rakuten Mobile partners with au (KDDI) for roaming in areas outside its own coverage zone, which significantly extends usability.

For most students living in or near cities, coverage is generally not an issue in day-to-day use.

## Foreign Residents: Can You Apply?

Yes — foreign residents in Japan are eligible to apply for Rakuten Mobile, as long as you have a valid residence card (在留カード, *zairyu card*) and a Japanese bank account or credit card for billing. You'll need a My Number card or residence card for identity verification during the application process.

## A Few Practical Things to Know

### Maximum Lines Per Person

One employee referral link can be used for up to **five lines**. This is useful if you're a student living in a shared house and want to help roommates or classmates sign up — everyone can benefit from the same referral campaign.

### No Long-Term Contract

Rakuten Mobile has no cancellation fees and no fixed contract period. If you're only in Japan for one or two academic years, you can sign up, enjoy the lower costs and point bonus, and cancel without penalty when you leave. This flexibility is a real advantage compared to carrier contracts that lock you in.

### Rakuten Points Work Like Cash

If you're already using Rakuten services — Rakuten Ichiba, Rakuten Travel, Rakuten Pay — the points you earn from signing up slot directly into your existing account. Even if you're new to the ecosystem, setting up a free Rakuten account before applying takes just a few minutes.
