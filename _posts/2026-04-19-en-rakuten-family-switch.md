## Is Rakuten Mobile a Good Fit for Foreign Residents?

This is a fair question. Here are a few things that foreign residents commonly ask:

### Can I sign up without Japanese citizenship?
Yes. Rakuten Mobile accepts foreign nationals living in Japan. You'll need a valid residence card (在留カード), a Japanese bank account or credit card, and a My Number card or other accepted ID. The application process can be completed online in most cases.

### Is the network coverage reliable?
Rakuten Mobile has significantly expanded its coverage over the past few years and now covers most urban and suburban areas well. In rural mountainous regions, coverage can be patchier, but for most people living and working in cities, it functions reliably. When outside the Rakuten network, the service roams on au's network, which provides additional backup coverage domestically.

### What about international roaming?
Rakuten Mobile does offer international roaming options, though the free unlimited data applies primarily within Japan. If you travel frequently, it's worth reviewing their international plans separately.

### Is the app and support available in English?
The Rakuten Mobile app and website are primarily in Japanese, which can be a hurdle. However, their support line does have some English-speaking assistance available, and the app itself is fairly navigable even with basic Japanese or a translation tool.

## Tips for Switching as a Family

If you're planning a group switch, here are a few practical suggestions to keep in mind:

1. **Coordinate your timing.** Since points are distributed starting four months after the referral login month, switching around the same time means everyone gets their point installments on a similar schedule.

2. **MNP is worth the extra step.** Switching via MNP (Mobile Number Portability) means you keep your existing phone number — and you get 14,000 points instead of 11,000. It takes a little more paperwork, but most people find it straightforward.

3. **Check your current contract.** Some major carriers have cancellation fees if you're mid-contract. Calculate whether the referral bonus and monthly savings offset any exit costs — in most cases, they do within just a few months.

4. **Make sure your phone is SIM-unlocked.** If your device was purchased through a carrier, it may be SIM-locked. You'll need to have it unlocked before switching. This can usually be done through your current carrier's app or website.

5. **Use one referral link per signup.** Each person joining needs to use the referral link during their own registration to qualify for the bonus points. It can't be applied retroactively.
